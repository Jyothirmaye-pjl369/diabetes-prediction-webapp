Understanding Diabetes & AI Analysis

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic condition where your body cannot properly process blood sugar (glucose), leading to high blood sugar levels that can cause serious health complications.

Risk Factors

Age, family history, obesity, high blood pressure, and sedentary lifestyle are major risk factors for developing Type 2 diabetes.

Diabetes Prevention & Management

Healthy Diet

  • Choose whole grains over refined carbs
  • Eat plenty of vegetables and fruits
  • Limit sugary drinks and snacks
  • Control portion sizes

Regular Exercise

  • Aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly
  • Include strength training exercises
  • Take regular walks after meals
  • Stay active throughout the day

Weight Management

  • Maintain a healthy BMI (18.5-24.9)
  • Focus on gradual weight loss
  • Monitor your progress regularly
  • Seek professional guidance if needed

Regular Check-ups

  • Monitor blood glucose levels
  • Check blood pressure regularly
  • Annual comprehensive health exams
  • Follow up with healthcare providers

Emergency Warning Signs

Severe Symptoms

  • Extreme thirst and frequent urination
  • Blurred vision or vision changes
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Slow-healing wounds or infections

When to Seek Help

  • Blood glucose > 300 mg/dL
  • Persistent vomiting
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Confusion or dizziness
  • Severe abdominal pain
